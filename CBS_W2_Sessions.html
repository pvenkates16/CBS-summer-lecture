How would you have rescued it?

What was actually done

Knight required emergency bailout of $400M. Acquired by Getco four months later. SEC fined $12M — 2.7% of the loss. No criminal charges. Industry continued operating without mandatory kill-switch requirements.

What was actually done

No. 13+ suicides. Decades of lost livelihoods. Many uncompensated. The system that caused the harm still runs. No individual accountability.

  • Audit independence: When an organisation's own system is the sole evidence for prosecuting the people who use that system, an independent technical audit must be mandatory before any prosecution.
  • Whistleblower protection: Subpostmasters repeatedly raised the alarm. Each was told individually they were the anomaly. A systemic reporting mechanism — where patterns across branches surface automatically — would have revealed the common cause.
  • Accountability question: Fujitsu still holds government contracts. Should participation in the evidence base for wrongful convictions be a permanent bar to public contracts?
